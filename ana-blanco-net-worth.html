Career and Major Achievements

Ana Blanco has had an illustrious career in journalism. Starting in radio with Cadena SER in 1980, she transitioned to television in 1989.

Her debut on Telemadrid alongside Agustín Bravo on the cultural program Zip-zap marked the beginning of her TV career. In 1990, she joined TVE and quickly became a staple on Telediario, anchoring various editions of the newscast.

Blanco’s dedication and skill have earned her numerous awards, including multiple ATV Awards and the prestigious Antena de Oro.

Her ability to cover major events, such as the 9/11 attacks, where she anchored live for over seven hours, showcases her expertise and resilience in journalism.

Role at Televisión Española

At Televisión Española, Blanco became synonymous with Telediario. Her role evolved over the years, making her the longest-serving anchor in the history of the newscast.

From the afternoon edition to weekend broadcasts, Blanco’s presence was a constant on Spanish TV screens. Her coverage extended beyond daily news; she hosted special programs for elections, breaking news, and historical anniversaries.

Major Events Covered by Ana Blanco

Blanco is known for her exceptional coverage of significant global events. On September 11, 2001, she provided live coverage of the World Trade Center attacks, delivering the news with clarity and composure. This broadcast remains one of the longest in Telediario history.
