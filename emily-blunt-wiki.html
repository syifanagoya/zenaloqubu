Emily Blunt Wiki-Lifestyle-Career

Blunt made her professional debut in Peter Hall's production of The Royal Family in November 2001, playing the granddaughter Gwen Cavendish to Judi Dench's Fanny Cavendish. "Peter Hall's directing and Anthony Ward's fantastic set combine with all of this to make The Royal Family a terrific night's entertainment," critic Tom Keatinge said, adding that "it gives a vehicle for acting of the highecaliberbre, with strong performances from the entire ensemble." The Evening Standard named Blunt "Best Newcomer" for her performance.

In 2002, she starred as Eugenie in Nicholas Wright's Vincent in Brixton at the National Theatre and as Juliet in Indhu Rubasingham's Romeo and Juliet at the Chichester Festival Theatre. Blunt made her movie debut in the British television drama Boudica in 2003, which is based on the life of the historical Celtic warrior-queen Boudica, who opposed the Romans. In the same year, she received critical acclaim for her portrayal of Queen Catherine Howard in the two-part British television drama Henry VIII.
